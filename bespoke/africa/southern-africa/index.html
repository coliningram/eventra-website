Where the Wilderness Begins.

From the Okavango Delta in Botswana to the wine estates of the Cape, the desert dunes of Namibia to the private islands of Mozambique — Southern Africa contains more extraordinary experiences per square kilometre than anywhere else on earth. Eventra places you inside all of it.

The Heart of Africa

Where safari was invented.
Where wilderness meets world-class.

Southern Africa is the heart of the continent's luxury travel offering. It is where safari was invented, where the Big Five still roam private conservancies larger than some countries, and where the transition from wilderness to world-class wine country takes less than an hour.

Our expert network of in-country specialists has spent decades building relationships with the lodges, guides, and private reserves that do not appear in standard search results — and cannot be booked through standard channels.

Where We Travel

Six Countries. One Region.

Botswana

The Delta.
Africa's last great wilderness.

The Okavango Delta floods annually to create a shifting landscape of channels, islands, and floodplains teeming with wildlife. Private concessions offer mokoro excursions and walking safaris in areas where no other vehicles operate.

South Africa

South Africa.
Safari, coast, and cellar.

Big Five safaris in the private reserves of Sabi Sand. The Cape winelands — Stellenbosch, Franschhoek, and Constantia. Cape Town itself — one of the world's great cities by any measure.

Namibia

Namibia.
The oldest desert on earth.

2.5 million people in a country the size of France and Germany combined. The Namib's red dunes at Sossusvlei. Etosha's salt pans. The Skeleton Coast — raw, remote, and unlike anywhere else.

Enquire →

Mozambique

Mozambique.
The Indian Ocean at its finest.

Nearly 2,500 kilometres of Indian Ocean coastline. The Bazaruto Archipelago and Quirimbas Islands offer some of the best diving in the world, alongside barefoot luxury lodges accessible only by light aircraft.

Enquire →

Zimbabwe

Zimbabwe.
The smoke that thunders.

Victoria Falls is one of the seven natural wonders of the world — a kilometre-wide curtain of water falling 108 metres into the Zambezi Gorge. Helicopter flights, sunset cruises, and luxury lodges positioned to make the most of the spectacle.

Enquire →

Zambia

Zambia.
Walking safari country.

Zambia is where the walking safari was born. The South Luangwa Valley offers intimate encounters with wildlife on foot, guided by some of the most experienced bush guides on the continent.
