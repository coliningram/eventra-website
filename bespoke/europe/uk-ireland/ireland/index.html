Wild Atlantic. Country House. Properly Experienced.

Ireland, for those who
want it properly.

Ireland’s hospitality is not performance — it is the real thing. The country house hotels of Connemara and Kerry are among the finest in Europe: places where the welcome is personal, the cooking is built around what grows and grazes locally, and the landscape provides all the drama anyone could ask for. The west coast is extraordinary on its own terms — the Cliffs of Moher, the karst limestone of the Burren, the Aran Islands, the long Atlantic light.

Eventra designs Irish journeys for clients who want the country without compromise. A driver-guide who knows every stone on the road. Hospitality at the Aviva Stadium that matches the occasion of an Ireland Test match. Private fishing on the rivers of County Mayo. The Wild Atlantic Way with somewhere worth stopping at every night.

The Wild Atlantic Way

Donegal to Cork. One of Europe’s great coastal drives.

The Wild Atlantic Way runs 2,500 kilometres from Donegal in the north to Cork in the south. The Cliffs of Moher at dusk, when the day-trippers have gone. The Aran Islands by ferry on a clear morning. The Dingle Peninsula on a day when the weather comes in off the Atlantic and the light turns extraordinary. Eventra designs Wild Atlantic Way itineraries built around private stays — the country houses and small hotels that make the difference between a drive and a journey.

Dublin & the Aviva

Dublin for the rugby. The Aviva and the atmosphere that comes with it.

Dublin for the rugby is unlike any other rugby experience in the world. The Aviva Stadium in Ballsbridge, the streets around Lansdowne Road before kick-off, the pubs of Baggot Street in the hours that follow — Ireland Test match atmosphere has no equivalent. Eventra has run hospitality programmes in Dublin for Six Nations weekends and international fixtures. We design the stay, the hospitality, and the programme around the match.

Country House Hotels

County Clare. County Kerry. Where hospitality is taken seriously.

Ireland’s finest country house hotels are a standard of their own. The houses of County Clare and County Kerry — some centuries-old estates, some purpose-built with the same ethos — offer a quality of welcome, cooking, and landscape that is hard to match anywhere in Europe. Private fishing on the rivers of Connemara. A round at Lahinch or Ballybunion with access arranged in advance. The kind of breakfast that makes the rest of the day irrelevant. Eventra knows these properties and builds itineraries around them.
