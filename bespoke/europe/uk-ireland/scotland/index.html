The Highlands. The Distilleries. The Courses.

Scotland at the level
it is capable of.

Scotland operates at a different pace to everywhere else in Britain. The Highlands reward those who move slowly — private sporting estates with fishing on rivers that have barely changed in centuries, stalking across glens that see few visitors. The whisky distilleries of Speyside and Islay, visited not via the visitor centre but through the doors that open for those who know to ask.

Eventra designs Scottish journeys for clients who want the country properly. The golf at St Andrews, Carnoustie, and Royal Dornoch. Edinburgh during the Festival or outside it. The Highlands at any time of year, from the long summer evenings to the clarity of a winter morning.

The Highlands

Vast, largely empty terrain. Private estates. The landscape that earns its reputation.

Scotland’s Highlands are among the most dramatic landscapes in Europe — a largely empty terrain of lochs, glens, and mountains that rewards those who move through it slowly. Private sporting estates offer fishing on rivers that have run largely unchanged for centuries, stalking across upland glens that see few visitors, and shooting of the standard that the landscape demands. Eventra arranges Highland journeys built around the access that takes years to establish.

Whisky Country

The Speyside and Islay distilleries. The private tastings that bear no resemblance to the tour.

The whisky distilleries of Speyside and Islay offer private tastings and access that bear no resemblance to the visitor centre experience. Eventra arranges distillery visits at the level where production managers open the warehouses, where casks are drawn directly, where the conversation moves from brand story to craft. The Speyside distillery trail. Islay by private charter for the peated malts. Scotland’s whisky country visited properly.

Golf

St Andrews, Carnoustie, Royal Dornoch. The game’s spiritual home.

Scotland invented golf and it remains the country where the game reaches its highest expression. The Old Course at St Andrews, where the game has been played for over six centuries. Carnoustie, the most demanding links in championship rotation. Royal Dornoch, set against the North Sea in a landscape that stops most people in their tracks. Eventra arranges tee times, caddies, and accommodation at courses that require more than a booking engine.

Edinburgh

The festival, the castle, the Old Town. Scotland’s capital at its fullest.

Edinburgh is one of Europe’s great capital cities — the castle above the Old Town, the Georgian symmetry of the New Town, the Fringe during August when the city becomes the world’s largest arts festival. Eventra arranges Edinburgh stays and programmes around the Festival, the Hogmanay celebrations, or independently of both. Private club access, the best tables, and accommodation that understands what the city is capable of.
