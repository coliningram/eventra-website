The World’s Most Visited Country

France rewards those who
move through it properly.

France has been the world’s most visited country for decades because it genuinely deserves the distinction. No other country offers the same breadth of experience at the same level — the world’s greatest city, the world’s finest wine regions, a coastline of extraordinary variety, and a food culture that has been the global benchmark for centuries.

The difference between a good France trip and an extraordinary one is access. The private cellar tasting at a first-growth Bordeaux château. The Michelin three-star that does not take outside reservations. The villa on Cap Ferrat that is never marketed publicly. The château in the Loire that opens its private apartments to guests Eventra has arranged. This is what we design.

France, Region by Region.

City

Paris

Paris rewards every season and every visit. It is a city of neighbourhoods, and understanding which one suits a particular client is the first thing Eventra does. The 7th arrondissement for classic Parisian grandeur, the Marais for art and architecture, Saint-Germain-des-Prés for literary cafe culture, the 16th for the Bois de Boulogne and some of the finest residential hotels in the city.

Beyond accommodation, Paris is about access. The table at a three-Michelin-starred restaurant that has a two-year waiting list for external reservations. The private tour of the Musée d’Orsay before it opens to the public. The couture appointment that does not appear on any agency’s list. Eventra designs Paris as a city of private access, not tourist itineraries.

Southern France

Provence

Provence in May or June — before the summer crowds arrive and before the lavender peaks — is one of the most beautiful places in Europe. The light is extraordinary. The markets in Apt, L’Isle-sur-la-Sorgue, and Arles are genuinely among the finest in France. The région’s rosé is not just the most consumed in the world; the best of it — from estates in the Luberon and the Var — is genuinely excellent.

Eventra designs Provence around the private mas or château that is the right base, the winemakers and chefs who will open their doors, and the villages that most visitors never find. The Luberon in spring. The Alpilles in autumn. The Camargue at any time of year for those who want something different from the postcard version of the south.

Loire Valley & The Riviera

The Loire & The Côte d’Azur

The Loire Valley holds the highest concentration of châteaux in France — Chambord, Chenonceau, Villandry, Azay-le-Rideau — and some of the most underrated wine appellations in the country. Eventra designs Loire itineraries that move beyond the famous câstles and into the private domains, the family domaines producing Vouvray and Saumur-Champigny, and the river itself, best experienced by private boat.

The Côte d’Azur — from Menton to Saint-Tropez — is the most glamorous stretch of coastline in the world and demands no justification. What it demands is the right access: the villa that is never marketed, the yacht berth that does not appear online, the restaurant in Cap d’Antibes that the locals go to instead of the ones in every guide. Eventra’s Riviera contacts are built over years of relationship, not research.
