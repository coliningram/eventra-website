Imperial Europe’s Capital

Austria holds the memory
of a great empire.

No country in Europe holds its imperial inheritance as gracefully as Austria. Vienna is the finest surviving example of a continental capital at full scale: the Kunsthistorisches Museum, the Staatsoper, the Naschmarkt, the Ringstraße, Schönbrunn. A city that takes its coffee, its music and its art seriously — and where knowing the right table in the right Kaffeehaus matters more than any guidebook recommendation.

Salzburg is baroque architecture, the Salzach River, and one of the great music festivals in the world. The Tyrol is the Alps at their Austrian best: Kitzbühel for skiing and the Hahnenkamm, Innsbruck for history and altitude, St Anton for serious skiing on the Arlberg. Eventra designs Austria for clients who want cultural depth alongside natural grandeur.

Vienna & Salzburg

Vienna and Salzburg.
Music, art and empire.

Vienna operates best when approached slowly. The Kunsthistorisches Museum requires a full day and rewards multiple. The Staatsoper — a private box, the right production, a pre-performance dinner at a restaurant that your concierge would not know to recommend — is one of the great cultural evenings in Europe. The Naschmarkt on a Saturday morning. The Café Central for coffee and the Wiener Zeitung. The cellar wine bars of the first district where Gümpoldskirchner is still served by the glass.

Salzburg rewards festival timing: July and August for the Salzburger Festspiele, when the city becomes the centre of the classical music world. At other times it is quieter, more beautiful and more itself — Mozart’s birthplace, the Festung Hohensalzburg above the river, the Getreidegasse and its handpainted signs. Eventra arranges both at the level each moment deserves.

The Tyrol

The Tyrol.
Austria’s Alpine finest.

The Austrian Tyrol is the Alps at their most characterful. Kitzbühel in January for the Hahnenkamm downhill — one of the great sporting events in Europe — and for the après-ski culture that has made the town a fixture on the international winter calendar. St Anton am Arlberg for skiing at the highest level, on terrain that rewards ability. Innsbruck for the combination of city and mountain that few places in Europe can match.

In summer, the Tyrol transforms: hiking trails through flower meadows, mountain biking above treeline, and the farmhouse accommodation culture that Austria does better than anywhere. Eventra designs Tyrol programmes for both seasons — including access to the Hahnenkamm weekend for clients who want to witness the finest Alpine downhill race in the world from the right vantage point.
